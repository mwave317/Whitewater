Our Mission

Dedicated to promoting healthy and active lifestyles and developing environmental stewardship, the USNWC is home to the world’s largest man-made whitewater river, offering premier outdoor activities including whitewater rafting and kayaking, flatwater kayaking, mountain biking, rock climbing, zip lines, a canopy tour, rope courses and various team building activities. The Day Pass is the most popular way to experience the USNWC, offering access to available activities on the day of a visit.

Come experience our Tours

Tours provide guided experiences, in smaller groups, on a scheduled basis. Since Tours are reservation-based, guests can enjoy the tour activities without having to wait in line. Each Tour is booked in advance and departs at a specified time.

Daily Activity Schedule

The trail system is constantly monitored and is opened or closed as conditions permit or require. Current status is posted at various locations with Red (closed) or Green (open) flags.

OUTDOOR ACTIVITIES

The USNWC’s 1,300 acres offers a wide variety of outdoor activities for all ages and skill levels. Guests can enjoy whitewater rafting and kayaking, flatwater kayaking, stand-up paddle boarding, rock climbing, zip lines, ropes courses, a canopy tour, and mountain biking on our 30+ mile trail system.

FACILITY & TRAIL MAP

The USNWC is situated on over 1,300 acres of woodlands, and contains over 40 miles of trail for hiking, mountain biking, and trail running.

HEALTHIER LIFESTYLE

Since 2006, the USNWC has been promoting access to the outdoors for all levels of participants through its 30+ land and water-based activities, outdoor instruction and certification programs, festivals, races, films, and various outdoor events throughout the year.

Choose one of three great tours

 

Canopy Tour

Explore the woodlands along the Catawba River and portions of the Historic Tuckaseegee Ford and Trail on the USNWC’s Canopy Tour. Participants are guided through the tree canopy traveling from platform to platform on a series of zips, sky bridges, cargo net climbs, rappels and other high adventure challenges. The tour reaches heights in excess of 60 feet in the hardwood trees that lace a 90 foot deep canyon before working its way through a wetlands delta and proceeding along the banks of the Catawba River. The Canopy Tour is available by reservation only. Click here to reserve now.

  • QUALIFIERS:Up to 275 lbs 4feet 8in tall
  • DIFFICULTY: Intermediate

Enjoy a 3-4 hour guided trip along the Catawba River with our Canopy Tour Pass. Participants will travel through the trees to 14 platforms via cargo net climbs, rappel stations, sky bridges, seven zip-lines and more.

Raft + Zip Tour

For those looking for a guided experience on water and land, look no further than Raft + Zip Tour. Guests enjoy both a 1.5 hour guided Canopy Tour through the trees next to the Catawba River, and a 1.5 hour Family Rafting or Adventure Rafting experience. Rafting and Zip Tour components can be sequenced as desired for the day of your visit. Raft + Zip Tours are available by reservation only during the months of March through October.

  • Please call 704-391-3900 for non-listed tours
  • QUALIFIERS: Taller than 4feet 8in tall
  • At least 11 years old and under 275 lbs
  • DIFFICULTY: Intermediate

Looking for adventure on the water and in the trees? Enjoy the best of both with the Raft + Zip Tour. Guests will venture through half of the USNWC’s Canopy Tour (roughly 1.5 hours), as well as an Adventure Rafting trip down the world’s largest manmade whitewater river.
